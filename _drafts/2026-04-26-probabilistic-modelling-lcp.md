## What is Least Cost Path? 
Least Cost Path (LCP) is an analytical method that has been used prevalently in archaeology to find out the possible paths taken by people in the past, from one point A to another point B on a map. 

## Why Probabilistic Modelling? 
Since LCP analyses have been done prevalently, there are also several known drawbacks and biases from LCP. (include citations of past research)
My biggest motivation for testing out this method of probabilistic modelling from Joseph Lewis (add citation) was when I was doing the regular LCP analyses for an internship, and realised that the resulting paths was very similar to what was obvious in the DEM model: paths that traverse through valleys and the flattest parts of the DEM, avoiding most elevation. This result is surely intuitive and makes a lot of sense when one is trying to model a path by foot. However, when I presented the results to my internship supervisor, I got the comment that "Of course, the result must be influenced by the DEM". As such, I started to delve deeper into understanding the biases and limitations of LCP.
